CNN Model Pruning

Extremely powerful AI models often require a lot of compute resources and runtime for forward inference. Model Pruning is one way to manage the trade off between model complexity & model efficiency. During Fall 2021 I worked on pruning a Deep CNN with around 600,000 trainable parameters through sparsification. I designed a bunch of prune strategies. The best pruned model has 97% sparsity and accuracy of 74.2% (uncompressed model accuracy = 76%). Thus we decreased our model parameters from 600k to 18k while limiting the loss in accuracy to only 2 points.

Keywords: #model_pruning, #CNN, #model_compression, #sparsity, #Tensorflow, #Keras

GRF predictor RNNs

Measurements of reaction force during a human gait have been traditionally performed in expensive lab setup. In Spring 2021 we worked on using LSTMs to predict the reaction force through IMU data. Because of the advent of wearable technologies IMU data can be gathered much easily. I worked on implementing Ray tune framework to run ASHA scheduler scheme to tune the hyperparameters on a grid search space. ASHA scheduler uses parallel model training and aggressive early-stopping techniques to reduce the tune & train time. You can see the sample prediction task of our model using various groups of IMU's vs the ground truth the figure on the right.

Keywords: #LSTM, #RNN, #Pytorch, #Ray_tune, #deep_learning, #hyperparameter_tuning, #ASHA_scheduler


Predicting Pollution using county infrastructure data

For the final project in my AI/ML class at Carnegie Mellon during Fall 2020, I tried to explore if a US county's infrastructure data can be utilized to predict its polution levels. The infrastructure data here includes power plants, fuel stations, prevelance of commercial spaces, length of roads etc. Given that not a single data source for all these infrastructure details exist, I carried out entity resolution. I tested various ML pipelines changing the models, preprocessing schema and also altering the prediction task between classification and regression. Robotic Arm Motion planning

In Fall 2020 I worked on trajectory optimization for a two-link Robot in a Gravity enabled environment. I utilized Matlab's fmincon method to find a trajectory which requires robot to expend least thermal cost of actuation. However the code can accept any cost function to optimize the trajectory over. The user can also add any additional constraints according to the environment and the task. For instance the gif towards the right shows comparison between the unconstrained optimization Vs the case where the root link can't swing beyond ±135° from the horizontal.

Keywords: #Matlab, #trajectory_optimization, #robot_dynamics, #constrained_optimization

Griot: Comprehensive Meeting Management Tool

On an average 15% of a professional's time is wasted in bad team meetings which fail to achieve the stated goals in the stated time. In Fall 2021 for the course on Product Management, I worked to identify the problems in Team Meetings and design a solution to arrest these problems and generate user value. Through customer interviews I realized that there are 3 key root causes for a bad meeting: a) No agenda has been setup b) Ineffective way to create and track action items c) No meeting summary is prepared. I envisioned a software tool called Griot to arrest these issues. Griot has a potential to save one hour of user's time every day! Checkout this link to a Compelling Pitch Deck for Griot including early stage prototypes. Also check out the Product Workbook for more details.

Benchmarking Snappiness measurements for Barcode Scanners

In Fall 2021, I worked with a team of Honeywell on creating procedures to benchmark the snappiness of barcode scanners. We want our benchmarking measurements to be repeatable (i.e. devoid of human vairance) and relatable (i.e. it measures what snappiness would be in real use cases). For this we adopted 2 phase design process. The first phase consists of ethnogrpahic studies of current use cases to formalize what snappiness means. The next step was to design the procedures that repatably measures a quantitative snappiness measurements. Check out our Presentation & detailed Report.

Keywords: #ethnography, #honeywell, #benchmarking, #key_performance_metric, #product_differentiation
